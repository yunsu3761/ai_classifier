Label: Cost_effective_low_carbon_steelmaking_technologies
Dimension: Energy_Efficiency_Improvement_Technologies
Description: Low-carbon ironmaking refers to a category of technologies that reduce carbon emissions across coke-making, sintering, and blast furnace processes through improvements in energy efficiency, fuel substitution, raw material structure innovation, hydrogen utilization, and next-generation process advancement. This classification includes technologies that lower carbon intensity while maintaining the conventional blast furnace ironmaking route, excluding full process transitions such as DRI-EAF.

These technologies directly reduce energy consumption and carbon emissions in sintering operations.
