Label: battery
Dimension: recycling
Description: Recycling is the entire process of recovering valuable metals and materials from end-of-life products, especially waste batteries and metal scrap, to either reuse them or input them for the production of new products. This process is becoming increasingly important with the proliferation of electric vehicles and energy storage systems (ESS) and can be broadly divided into three stages: pre-treatment, post-treatment, and material remanufacturing. In particular, effective extraction of precious metals such as lithium, nickel, and cobalt is essential in waste battery recycling, along with techniques to convert these into new battery materials. For example, one method for producing cathode active materials for lithium-ion batteries involves the grinding of a mixture that includes lithium compounds, nickel compounds, manganese compounds, and cobalt compounds. Additionally, the technology for extracting lithium from brine involves generating lithium leachate using an ion exchange reactor, which plays a crucial role in recovering lithium from waste batteries. These technologies contribute to quality enhancement through impurity removal, high-purity lithium compound production, and recrystallization processes. Such recycling processes minimize environmental impacts through sustainable resource management and play a significant role in establishing new raw material supply chains, emphasizing the promotion of reuse of metals and compounds. This recycling technology primarily focuses on the recovery and recycling of waste batteries and metal scraps, differing from existing technologies related to waste disposal.
