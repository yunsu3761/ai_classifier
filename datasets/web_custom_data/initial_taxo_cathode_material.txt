Label: cathode_material
Dimension: technology_classification
Description: Cathode Material: The cathode active materials in secondary batteries store and release electrical energy through the intercalation and de-intercalation of lithium ions. These materials are engineered for various applications, including electric vehicles (EVs), energy storage systems (ESS), and portable IT devices. Their performance is dictated by several factors, such as the chemical composition, crystal structure, nickel content, surface coating, and doping technologies. This field encompasses the comprehensive research and development of cathode materials for lithium-ion batteries. It also includes studies on various compositions and manufacturing techniques aimed at enhancing battery safety and efficiency. Advancements in these technologies are essential for next-generation energy storage solutions like EVs and ESS. This research presents various compositions and structures for lithium-ion battery cathode active materials, with a particular focus on manufacturing methods involving artificial graphite and metal composites, the utilization of high-nickel cathode materials, and recycling technologies for spent cathode materials through thermal treatment. For instance, reusing recovered active materials by applying heat treatment and adding lithium precursors contributes to the development of sustainable battery technologies. Furthermore, techniques regarding the fabrication of composite cathode materials play a critical role in enhancing the energy density and cycle life of batteries. Such technologies can maximize battery performance by establishing optimal electrode structures and conductive networks.
