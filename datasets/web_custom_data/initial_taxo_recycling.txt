Label: recycling
Dimension: technology_classification
Description: Recycling: Recycling is the comprehensive process of recovering valuable metals and materials from end-of-life products—particularly spent batteries and metal scrap—to reuse them or reintegrate them into the production of new products. This process is becoming increasingly important with the proliferation of electric vehicles (EVs) and energy storage systems (ESS), and is broadly divided into pre-treatment, post-treatment, and material remanufacturing stages. These recycling processes contribute significantly to minimizing environmental impact through sustainable resource management and establishing new raw material supply chains, with a focus on promoting the reuse of metals and compounds. Recycling technologies primarily focus on the recovery and recycling of spent batteries and metal scrap, and they differ from conventional waste treatment technologies. Specifically, in spent battery recycling, technologies that effectively extract precious metals such as lithium, nickel, and cobalt and convert them into new battery materials are essential. For example, as a method for manufacturing cathode active materials for lithium-ion batteries, there is a process of grinding a mixture containing lithium, nickel, manganese, and cobalt compounds. Additionally, technology for extracting lithium from brine involves using an ion-exchange reactor to produce a lithium eluate, which plays a critical role in recovering lithium from spent batteries. These technologies contribute to enhancing quality through impurity removal, the production of high-purity lithium compounds, and recrystallization processes.
