Label: solid_electrolyte
Dimension: technology_classification
Description: Solid Electrolyte: Electrolytes that conduct lithium ions in a solid state are the core materials of All-Solid-State Batteries (SSBs). These electrolytes facilitate the movement of ions within a solid lattice or amorphous structure, offering higher stability and safety compared to liquid electrolytes. The efficiency and lifespan of a battery depend on various performance factors of the electrolyte, such as interfacial properties with electrodes, ionic conductivity, and mechanical stability. The primary challenges facing solid-state electrolytes are cost-effectiveness and the feasibility of mass production. Related patents focus on the composition and structural diversity of solid-state electrolytes, including, for example, new oxide-based solid electrolytes, high-ionic-conductivity polymer electrolytes, and composite solid electrolyte films. In particular, lithium thioborate compositions or lithium nitride-based oxides play a critical role in enhancing lithium-ion conductivity. Furthermore, solid electrolyte systems combined with organic solvents maximize ionic conductivity while ensuring mechanical stability. These technologies contribute to maximizing performance in various application fields associated with the commercialization of all-solid-state batteries.
