Label: battery
Dimension: solid_electrolyte
Description: The electrolyte that conducts lithium ions in the solid state is a key material for solid-state batteries (SSB), as it enables ion movement within a solid lattice or amorphous structure, providing higher stability and safety compared to liquid electrolytes. This electrolyte influences the battery's efficiency and lifespan depending on various performance factors such as interfacial characteristics with electrodes, ionic conductivity, and mechanical stability. Relevant patents focus on the composition and structural diversity of solid electrolytes, including new oxide-based solid electrolytes, high ionic conductivity polymer electrolytes, and composite solid electrolyte films. In particular, lithium thioborate compositions and lithium nitride-based oxides play a crucial role in enhancing lithium ion conductivity. Furthermore, solid electrolyte systems combined with organic solvents maximize ionic conductivity while ensuring mechanical stability. Such technologies contribute to maximizing performance in various application fields related to the commercialization of solid-state batteries. However, the cost-effectiveness and scalability of solid electrolytes remain challenges to be addressed.
